Verilog & Netlist Training Datasets to Accelerate AI for Chip Design

We provide over 100B tokens of novel Verilog and SystemVerilog code. The training data includes RTL and testbenches across GPUs, TPUs, ASICs, FPGAs, NVIDIA DLAs, and AMD CDNA architectures, including systolic arrays, tensor cores, vector ALUs, and MAC units.

The dataset covers difficult chip design challenges and architectures:

  • Floorplanning: Optimizing block placement to minimize area, wirelength, and congestion
  • Timing closure: Meeting setup/hold constraints across millions of paths under PVT variation
  • Clock domain crossing: Synchronizing signals between asynchronous clock domains
  • Power-aware RTL: Reducing dynamic and leakage power while maintaining performance
  • Systolic arrays & tensor cores: Parallel matrix operations for ML accelerators
  • SIMD & vector pipelines: Single-instruction, multiple-data execution across wide datapaths
  • Von Neumann & Harvard architectures: Shared vs. split instruction/data memory hierarchies
  • VLIW & superscalar cores: Exploiting instruction-level parallelism through static and dynamic scheduling
  • Dataflow architectures: Token-driven execution where operations fire as operands become available
  • Network-on-chip: Scalable on-chip interconnects for multi-core communication
  • Custom DMA engines: Efficient data movement between memory hierarchies
